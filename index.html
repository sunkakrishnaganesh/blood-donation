Why Your Donation Matters

Blood donations are essential for surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and traumatic injuries. A single donation can be separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets to help multiple patients.

Emergency Situations

Critical for accident victims and surgical patients who need immediate blood transfusions to survive.

Chronic Illnesses

Patients with conditions like sickle cell anemia need regular blood transfusions throughout their lives.

Medical Procedures

Essential for complex surgeries, organ transplants, and cancer treatments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Generally, donors must be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some areas), weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good health. There are some additional eligibility requirements based on medical history, travel, and medications. All donors must pass a health screening on the day of donation.

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donors can donate more frequently - up to 24 times per year. Plasma donors can donate every 28 days. The waiting period between donations allows your body to replenish its blood supply.

You may feel a brief pinch when the needle is inserted, but most donors report little to no pain during the donation process. The actual donation typically takes about 8-10 minutes for a whole blood donation.

The entire process takes about an hour from start to finish. This includes registration, a health history questionnaire, a mini-physical, the donation itself (which takes about 8-10 minutes), and refreshments afterward.

Yes, donating blood is very safe. All equipment used is sterile, disposable, and used only once. There is no risk of contracting any infectious disease from donating blood. Our staff are trained professionals who follow strict safety protocols.
